What are the plotlines of Blue Lock Chapter 245?

Blue Lock is a sports manga that revolves around the theme of egoism and the quest to become the best striker in the world. The plot follows the journey of Yoichi Isagi, a high school soccer player who joins a radical training program called Blue Lock, where 300 strikers compete to become the ultimate striker. The manga explores the psychological and physical aspects of soccer, as well as the characters’ motivations, struggles, and growth. The manga also features various plot twists, surprises, and cliffhangers, as well as intense and thrilling matches, that keep the readers hooked and engaged.

The manga is divided into several arcs, each focusing on a different stage of the Blue Lock program and the development of the characters. The main arcs are:

  • The Selection Arc: This arc covers the first phase of the Blue Lock program, where the 300 strikers are divided into 11 teams and have to play against each other in a round-robin tournament. The top 11 strikers from each team will advance to the next phase, while the rest will be eliminated. This arc introduces the main characters and their personalities, as well as the rules and goals of the Blue Lock program. This arc also shows the first match between Team Z, led by Isagi, and Team V, led by Rin, which ends in a draw.
  • The Survival Arc: This arc covers the second phase of the Blue Lock program, where the 121 remaining strikers are divided into 11 teams again and have to play against each other in a knockout tournament. The winning team will advance to the next phase, while the losing team will be eliminated. This arc shows the development of the teamwork and the bonds between the members of Team Z, as well as the rivalry and the conflicts between the other teams. This arc also shows the second match between Team Z and Team V, which ends in a victory for Team Z.
  • The World Arc: This arc covers the third phase of the Blue Lock program, where the 11 members of Team Z have to play against the U-20 national teams of various countries, such as France, Brazil, Germany, and Spain. The goal of this phase is to test the skills and the potential of the Blue Lock strikers against the world-class players. This arc shows the growth and the challenges of the Blue Lock strikers, as well as the reactions and the opinions of the international players and the media.
