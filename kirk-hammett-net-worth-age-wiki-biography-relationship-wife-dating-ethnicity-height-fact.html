Biography and Early Life

Kirk Hammett was born on November 18, 1962, in San Francisco, California, and spent his formative years in the town of El Sobrante. His unique heritage includes a Filipino mother, Teofila “Chefela” Oyao, and a father, Dennis L. Hammett, with roots in Britain, Germany, Scotland, and Ireland. Hammett attended De Anza High School in Richmond, California, where he formed a lasting friendship with Les Claypool of Primus.

Musical Journey and Early Influences

Hammett’s fascination with horror traces back to the late 1960s when, after injuring his arm in a childhood scuffle, his parents introduced him to television, and he discovered “The Day of the Triffids.” This marked the beginning of his interest in horror, fueled by his brother’s Frankenstein toys and a penchant for horror magazines. However, his musical journey began when he delved into his brother Rick’s extensive record collection, leading him to play the guitar seriously at the age of 15.

Notable among his early guitars was a modest Montgomery Ward catalog special, later replaced by a 1974 Gibson Flying V after experimenting with different parts on a 1978 Fender Stratocaster.
