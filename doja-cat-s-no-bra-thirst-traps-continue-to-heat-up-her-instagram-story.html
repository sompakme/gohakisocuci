Doja Cat's No-Bra Thirst Traps Continue To Heat Up Her Instagram Story

Doja Cat's fans are eagerly awaiting the arrival of her Hellmouth album, even though the singer has been consistently trolling them about it on social media. From stating that the project is "French conceptual experimental country/bohemian fusion with the essence of blue-grass," to asserting that she's planning to "quit music" due to the negativity it's bringing into her life, the Planet Her hitmaker's Twitter feed has been all over the place as of late. Thankfully, things are much clearer on Doja's Instagram page, where she's had followers drooling over her not-so-subtle thirst traps.

The salacious snapshots show off the 27-year-old's recently altered breasts, which she previously said are now a 32C. Viewers can clearly see them sitting under her sheer, cropped sweater, looking perfectly healed weeks after she initially announced her surgery. Around the same time as her augmentation, Doja also had liposuction and joked about having her "cl*t bedazzled" in a comedic tweet. The "Woman" songstress was candid (and sometimes silly) while answering questions about the procedures, and now, she's proudly showing off her new body for the world on her Story.

Earlier in the week, she shared some similar photos, which saw her rocking MSCHF's Big Red Boots along with her see-through sweater. On Friday (April 21), Doja returned with more, this time switching up her look to add yellow-tinted sunglasses sitting on her smokily-beaten face. While one photo certainly would've been enough to satisfy most, the blonde beauty blessed IG with two more. The second shows her cooly exhaling smoke while flaunting her abs for the camera.

To end the show, Doja Cat gave us another view of her outfit, this one from the side, further proving how perky her chest is thanks to her operation. As we continue the countdown to Hellmouth, the Californian surprised music lovers last weekend by hopping on the remix of SZA's "Kill Bill," which she obviously killed. The artist's second collaboration comes after DC promised to bring better-than-ever-before rap bars on her upcoming album.
