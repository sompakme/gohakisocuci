What’s the distinction between caulk and sealant?

Elasticity. The primary distinction between a caulk and a sealant is elasticity. When dry, caulks are fairly rigid and are intended for use in areas with minimal expansion and contraction. Sealants are made of flexible materials, most commonly silicone, and are therefore ideal for areas prone to expansion and contraction.
