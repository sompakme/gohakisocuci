How to Stream the 2024 NHL Stadium Series Hockey Games Online for Free

If you purchase an independently reviewed product or service through a link on our website, The Hollywood Reporter may receive an affiliate commission.

This weekend’s 2024 NHL Stadium Series pits four East Coast hockey teams against one another at New Jersey’s MetLife Stadium. Both games air on ABC and can be streamed online without cable on ESPN+, among other ways to watch online.

On Saturday, the Philadelphia Flyers take on the New Jersey Devils, with a start time of 5 p.m. PT/8 p.m. ET. The Jonas Brothers will perform before the game at 3:30 p.m. PT/6:30 p.m. ET and during the second intermission.

The New York Rangers will battle the New York Islanders on Sunday, with puck drop at 12 p.m. PT/3 p.m. ET.

If you want to catch all of the hockey action, keep reading to find out how to watch the NHL Stadium Series with and without a cable subscription.

How to Watch the 2024 NHL Stadium Series Without Cable

Since the NHL Stadium Series broadcasts on ABC, the games can be streamed on internet-based alternative cable services, like DirecTV Stream, Fubo, Hulu + Live TV and others. Some services even offer free trials, so if you act fast and sign up now, you can watch the outdoor hockey games for free.

Here’s how you can watch the NHL Stadium Series without cable below:

ESPN

ESPN+

If you’d like to livestream the entire NHL Stadium Series, then ESPN+ is the best pick. The streaming service has both games available to stream, along with the Jonas Brothers’ performances. Packages start at $10.99 per month.

Along with ESPN+, the Disney Bundle Trio comes with the ad-supported versions of Hulu and Disney+ for $14.99 per month. This is nearly 45 percent less than if you were to subscribe to each service individually. If you’d like to go ad-free, the Disney Bundle Trio goes for $24.99 per month.

DirecTV

DirecTV Stream

Watch ABC with DirecTV Stream for the NHL Stadium Series, along with more than 75 other channels — such as NBC, ESPN, Fox, CBS, AMC, Bravo, CNBC, Disney Channel and much more.

The streamer has a free five-day trial available, but that’s more than enough time to watch the entire NHL Stadium Series on ABC. Afterward, you can either cancel the service or keep watching, with prices starting as low as $69.99 for the entry-level Entertainment package. Other plans offer more than 105-150 channels, with prices ranging from $84.99 to $159.99 per month.

For a limited time, new subscribers can get a Visa gift card worth up to $400; learn more here.

Fubo

Fubo

If you’d like to watch the entire NHL Stadium Series live on ABC — along with more than 170 other news, entertainment and sports channels — go with a subscription to Fubo. The streaming service starts at $79.99 per month, while you can record over 1,000 hours of TV shows, movies, games and more. Fubo is available to watch on your smartphone, tablet or TV too.

Additionally, other plans include the Elite package (with more than 250 channels for $89.99 per month) and the Premier tier that comes with nearly 300 channels, NFL RedZone, Showtime and 4K quality for $99.99 per month.

If you want to watch both NHL Stadium Series games live for free, the online TV streaming service offers a seven-day free trial for new subscribers.

Hulu

Hulu + Live TV

To watch ABC online, a subscription to Hulu + Live TV is another fantastic option. The streaming service has access to more than 90 live channels — like BET, CNN, Food Network and more — starting at $76.99 per month and comes with Hulu’s entire streaming library, as well as Disney+ and ESPN+.

In addition to live TV, Hulu + Live TV lets subscribers watch originals from Hulu, Marvel Studios, Lucasfilm (Star Wars), The Muppets Studio and Pixar Animation, as well as ESPN+ for original sports programming and live sports from Formula 1, UFC, MLB and other sports leagues. It includes unlimited cloud DVR, too. Unfortunately, Hulu doesn’t offer a free trial at this time.

Sling

Sling

Sling is one of the cheapest options for new live TV streaming subscribers who want to watch the NHL Stadium Series on ABC. The Sling Blue plan is only $22.50 per month for the first month ($45 per month afterward) and includes both networks.

Sling Blue also features The Discovery Channel, Fox News, TBS, Bravo, MSNBC and much more.

How to Watch the 2024 NHL Stadium Series With Cable

The NHL Stadium Series airs on the broadcast network ABC in the United States. You can watch through your cable TV provider, on ABC.com or the ABC mobile app with your cable TV account login.

Where to Buy 2024 NHL Stadium Series Tickets Online

Want to watch the game in person? You can find last-minute tickets for the NHL Stadium Series at Vivid Seats, StubHub, Ticket Liquidator, Ticket Network, GameTime and Ticketmaster, as well as NHL.com for more info. Ticket prices vary, depending on where you’d like to sit in MetLife Stadium.
