Biography

John Blaq (born Kasadha John on July 16, 1996) is a talented Ugandan singer and songwriter recognized for his distinctive dancehall and afrobeat musical blend. 

Since entering the music scene in 2018, he has consistently delivered hit singles, earning locally and internationally acclaim. Among his notable tracks are Tukwatagane, Sweet Love, and Kiboko. 

John Blaq has also made a mark through successful collaborations with popular Ugandan artists such as Vinka, Sheebah Karungi, and Fik Fameica, solidifying his position as one of the most sought-after entertainers in the country.

Early Life & Education

Born on July 16, 1996, in Jinja, Uganda, John Blaq is the child of Musa Waiswa Zodolo and Annet Nabirye. 

His formative years were spent in the Bweyogerere neighbourhood of Kampala alongside his siblings Azawi and Katumba Kasadha. 

John Blaq attended Lwanda Primary School and Hasan Tourabi Primary School for his primary education. Subsequently, in 2012, he pursued his secondary education at Bweyogerere Secondary School, achieving success in both the UCE and UACE examinations in 2016 and 2018, respectively.

Career

John Blaq‘s venture into the music industry commenced during his teenage years when he actively participated in various high school music competitions. During this period, he unearthed his passion for music and recognized his innate talent. 

Fueled by a strong desire to turn his love for music into a profession, Blaq dedicated substantial effort to refining his skills and shaping his distinct musical style. In 2018, he marked his musical debut with the successful single More Fire, quickly establishing himself as a promising new artist. However, his second single, Tukwatagane, truly delighted Blaq‘s artistic journey.

Following his initial success, John Blaq created a series of hit singles, earning significant acclaim and popularity. Some standout pieces in his repertoire include Sweet Love, Makanika, and Nekwataako. Collaborating with esteemed figures in the Ugandan music scene, such as Vinka, Sheebah Karungi, and Ykee Benda, further solidified Blaq’s standing as a prominent figure in the local music landscape.
